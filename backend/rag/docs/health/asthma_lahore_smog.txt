Asthma and Lahore Smog — Clinical Guidance Summary

People with asthma are highly sensitive to PM2.5 and PM10 during Lahore smog season
(November–January). When AQI exceeds 100, reduce outdoor activity. Above 150,
avoid exertion outdoors. Above 200, remain indoors with windows closed.

Always carry a rescue inhaler (salbutamol). Use a controller medication as prescribed.
N95 or KN95 masks reduce particulate inhalation during unavoidable outdoor exposure.

Walking or cycling in heavy traffic corridors increases dose compared to car travel
with windows closed and recirculated air. Prefer lower-traffic routes when possible.

Warm fluids, adequate hydration, and avoiding cold dry air help reduce airway irritation.
Seek emergency care for severe wheezing, chest tightness, or blue lips.

This information supports self-care decisions and does not replace a pulmonologist visit.
