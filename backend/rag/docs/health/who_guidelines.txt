WHO Air Pollution Health Guidelines (Summary)

Short-term exposure to fine particulate matter (PM2.5) increases respiratory symptoms,
cardiovascular stress, and hospital admissions. Sensitive groups include children,
elderly adults, and people with asthma or chronic lung disease.

When AQI exceeds 150 (Unhealthy), limit prolonged outdoor exertion. Above 200
(Very Unhealthy), all groups should avoid outdoor activity. N95 or equivalent
respirators reduce inhalation of fine particles when outdoor exposure is unavoidable.

Patients with asthma should keep rescue inhalers accessible, monitor peak flow,
and follow their action plan during smog episodes. Indoor air can be improved
with HEPA filtration and keeping windows closed during peak pollution hours.

Medical advice should always be personalized based on age, comorbidities, and
medication use. Consult a physician for severe symptoms including chest pain,
persistent cough, or breathing difficulty.

Reference: WHO ambient air quality guidelines emphasize reducing PM2.5 exposure
to protect public health in high-pollution urban environments such as Lahore.
