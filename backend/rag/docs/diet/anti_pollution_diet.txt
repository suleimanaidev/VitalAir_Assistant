Anti-Pollution Diet Research (Summary)

Vitamin C and vitamin E support antioxidant defenses against oxidative stress
from air pollution. Citrus fruits, berries, and leafy greens provide vitamin C.

Omega-3 fatty acids from walnuts, flaxseed, and fish may reduce inflammatory
responses linked to particulate exposure. Ginger and turmeric have anti-inflammatory
properties and are commonly recommended in warm teas during smog season.

Hydration with warm fluids helps maintain mucociliary clearance in the respiratory
tract. Green tea contains polyphenols that may support lung health.

Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, kale) and foods rich in beta-carotene support
general immune resilience. Avoid excessive fried and processed foods during
high-pollution days when systemic inflammation may be elevated.

Dietary recommendations complement — not replace — medical care and exposure
reduction strategies during Lahore smog episodes.
